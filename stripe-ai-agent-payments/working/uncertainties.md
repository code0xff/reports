# Uncertainties

- Actual transaction volume, revenue contribution, authorization rates, chargeback rates, and repeat usage for Stripe-enabled AI-agent purchases are not public.
- ACP/SPT adoption outside OpenAI, Stripe's announced AI-platform partners, and early ACS merchants may remain weak or uneven.
- Liability allocation for mistaken, fraudulent, prompt-injected, or unauthorized agent purchases is likely to evolve through card-network rules, processor policies, and jurisdiction-specific consumer protection law.
- SPT, AP2 mandates, network tokenization, and x402 solve overlapping but different parts of the authorization problem; no single standard has clear market dominance as of 2026-05-03.
- Stablecoin and wallet infrastructure may matter for cross-border or machine-to-machine agent payments, but mainstream retail adoption timing remains uncertain.
- Public evidence still over-indexes on vendor announcements and partnership press releases because agentic commerce is new.
